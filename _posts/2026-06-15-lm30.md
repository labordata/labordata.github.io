[Labordata](https://labordata.bunkum.us) now has
[LM 30 data](https://labordata.bunkum.us/lm30). These are forms that union
officers and employees are supposed to submit to the Department of Labor if they
have a financial interest or dealing that could pose a conflict of interest.

I've not priortized this data previously, but I had to do some major refactors
of the scrapers of the [LM 10](https://labordata.bunkum.us/lm10),
[20, and 21](https://labordata.bunkum.us/lm20) data (which are now working
again), and after those changes it was easy to add the LM 30 data, so I have.

Speaking of the LM 20 and 21 data, I was thrilled to see that data used by the
[Economic Policy Institute](https://www.epi.org) and
[LaborLab](https://laborlab.us) in their
[report estimating that union avoidance is a \$1.5 billiion dollar industry](https://www.epi.org/publication/u-s-employers-spend-more-than-1-5-billion-annually-on-union-avoidance/).

If you have used any data from the Labordata project for external publications
or internal analyses, I would be very grateful if you could share that with me!
